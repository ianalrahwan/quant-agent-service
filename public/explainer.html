HOW THE SCANNER SCORES RIVN (RIVIAN)

These four metrics measure the mechanical flows in the options market that create volatility. They answer: is the options market structure set up to amplify or dampen moves in this stock?

1. DEALER GAMMA (GEX) — Score: 68

What it measures: Are options dealers positioned in a way that will amplify or dampen the next move in RIVN?

"When dealers are short gamma, they have to sell into declines and buy into rallies. They amplify moves. That's the mechanical flow that creates the volatility events most people aren't positioned for." — Cem Karsan

The Intuition

When you buy a call or put, a market maker (dealer) sells it to you. To stay hedged, the dealer buys or sells shares of the stock. Gamma is how fast that hedge changes as the stock moves. When dealers are short gamma, every move forces them to trade in the same direction as the move — amplifying it.

How We Calculate It for RIVN

Step 1: Pull RIVN's full options chain — every call and put across the nearest 3 expirations.
Step 2: For each option contract, compute its Black-Scholes gamma:
gamma = N'(d1) / (S × σ × √T) where: S = RIVN spot price (~$13) K = strike price of this contract σ = implied volatility of this contract T = time to expiry in years d1 = [ln(S/K) + (r + σ²/2) × T] / (σ × √T) N'(x) = standard normal PDF = e^(-x²/2) / √(2π)
Step 3: For each contract, estimate the dealer's gamma exposure:
contract_gex = -1 × gamma × open_interest × 100 × spot_price The -1 is because dealers are assumed SHORT what customers are LONG. × 100 because each contract = 100 shares. × spot_price to convert to dollar gamma.
Step 4: Sum across ALL contracts (calls + puts, all strikes, all expirations):
Total GEX = Σ contract_gex = -$0.06 Billion

Negative = dealers are net short gamma.

Step 5: Normalize to a 0-1 score using a sigmoid:
normalized = -GEX / (|GEX| + 100,000,000) score = 0.5 + normalized × 0.5 GEX = -60,000,000 normalized = 60M / (60M + 100M) = 0.375 score = 0.5 + 0.375 × 0.5 = 0.688 → 68

A score of 50 = neutral. Above 50 = dealers short gamma (amplifying moves). Below 50 = dealers long gamma (dampening moves). RIVN at 68 = moderately short gamma.

2. TERM STRUCTURE — Score: 68

What it measures: Is near-term implied volatility higher or lower than longer-term IV? This tells you if the market sees risk as imminent or distant.

"When the front of the curve inverts over the back — when near-term vol exceeds longer-term vol — the market is telling you it sees an immediate risk that hasn't been resolved." — Cem Karsan

The Intuition

Normally, options further out in time cost more (higher IV) because there's more uncertainty — this is contango. When near-term IV flips above far-term IV, that's backwardation — the market is saying "danger is NOW, not later." For a long vol play, backwardation means your near-term options are worth more.

How We Calculate It for RIVN

Step 1: Get RIVN's options chain for the nearest and farthest available expirations. For example:

We find the call strike closest to RIVN's spot price at each expiration.

Step 2: Compute the ratio:
ratio = near_term_IV / far_term_IV ratio = 0.725 / 0.691 = 1.05

> 1.0 = backwardation (near > far)

< 1.0 = contango (near < far)

Step 3: Convert to score:
Ratio 1.05 is between 1.0 and 1.15 (mild backwardation) score = 0.5 + (1.05 - 1.0) / 0.15 × 0.5 score = 0.5 + 0.33 × 0.5 = 0.667 → 68

3. VANNA EXPOSURE — Score: 89

What it measures: How much will dealer hedging flows move the stock when volatility itself changes?

"Vanna is the hidden force. When volatility moves, deltas shift, and dealers have to re-hedge. That re-hedging creates the flows that amplify the original move. It's a feedback loop most people don't see." — Cem Karsan

The Intuition

Vanna = dDelta/dVol — how much an option's delta changes when implied vol changes. If a dealer is hedged at delta 0.30 and vol spikes, the delta might jump to 0.45. Now the dealer needs to buy more shares to stay hedged. Multiply this across thousands of contracts and you get massive forced buying or selling.

Negative net vanna = when vol rises, dealers must SELL → pushes stock down further → vol rises more → feedback loop.

How We Calculate It for RIVN

Step 1: For each option contract, compute Black-Scholes vanna:
vanna = -d2 × N'(d1) / σ where: d1 = [ln(S/K) + (r + σ²/2)T] / (σ√T) d2 = d1 - σ√T N'(d1) = standard normal PDF at d1
Step 2: Aggregate across all contracts (weighted by open interest):
contract_vanna = -1 × vanna × open_interest × 100 (Negative because dealers are short what customers are long)
Step 3: Sum everything:
Net Vanna = Σ contract_vanna = -3,589,500 (-3589.5K)

Large negative number = when vol rises, dealers will be forced to sell aggressively, amplifying the move.

Step 4: Normalize to score:
normalized = -(-3,589,500) / (|-3,589,500| + 1,000,000) normalized = 3,589,500 / 4,589,500 = 0.782 score = 0.5 + 0.782 × 0.5 = 0.891 → 89

RIVN's vanna score of 89 is STRONG — there is significant vol-driven hedging flow potential. If vol spikes, expect amplified moves from dealer re-hedging.

4. CHARM FLOWS — Score: 100

What it measures: How much forced dealer re-hedging will happen just from the passage of time?

"Every day that passes, options decay, deltas shift, and dealers must adjust. Near expiration, charm accelerates — the forced re-hedging creates predictable flow patterns that move the underlying." — Cem Karsan

The Intuition

Charm = dDelta/dTime — how much an option's delta changes with each passing day. As expiration approaches, OTM options lose delta fast (heading toward 0) and ITM options gain delta fast (heading toward 1). Dealers must constantly re-hedge these shifting deltas, creating daily flows that move the stock.

Near expiration with high open interest = massive daily flows = charm on steroids.

How We Calculate It for RIVN

Step 1: For each option contract, compute Black-Scholes charm:
charm = -N'(d1) × [2rT - d2σ√T] / (2Tσ√T) where: r = risk-free rate (0.05) T = time to expiry in years All other terms same as above
Step 2: Aggregate across all contracts:
contract_charm = -1 × charm × open_interest × 100
Step 3: Sum everything:
Net Charm = Σ contract_charm = 35,603,600 (35603.6K)

Massive magnitude = huge daily re-hedging flows. The sign tells you the direction, but for scoring we care about the magnitude — bigger = more forced flow = more potential for amplified moves.

Step 4: Normalize (using absolute magnitude):
magnitude = |35,603,600| = 35,603,600 normalized = 35,603,600 / (35,603,600 + 100,000) = 0.997 score = 0.997 → 100

RIVN's charm score of 100 is maximum — there is an enormous amount of time-decay-driven re-hedging happening. This is likely because RIVN has very high open interest near expiration (weekly options with lots of retail activity).
